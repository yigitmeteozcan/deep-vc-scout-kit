## Fund name
Baltic Industrial SaaS Fund (Example)

## Stage
Pre-seed to Seed. First check €100k–€500k. Follow-on to €1.5M in Seed round.

## Geography
Primary: Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Finland
Secondary: Turkish-Baltic diaspora founders building for European industrial markets
Also tracking: Polish and Romanian B2B SaaS with industrial focus

## Sectors
1. Industrial operations software (maintenance, scheduling, asset management)
2. Logistics and fleet management software for mid-market operators
3. Energy operations software (grid balancing, demand flexibility, energy procurement)
4. Supply chain visibility and procurement tools for manufacturers
5. Quality management and compliance software for regulated industries

## Check size
€150k–€400k initial. Pro-rata follow-on up to €1.5M. Target 8-12% ownership.

## Business models you prefer
- B2B SaaS with annual contracts
- Software with embedded services for industrial onboarding
- Usage-based pricing tied to operational metrics (assets, users, shifts)
- Platform models where industrial data creates network effects over time

## Must-have signals
1. Founder with industrial domain expertise or prior work in the target sector (not just "passion for industry")
2. At least one paid pilot or LOI from an industrial buyer — not just verbal interest
3. Clear workflow replacement — software that replaces something specific the buyer does today (spreadsheet, paper log, phone call, legacy system)
4. Baltic or Nordic market entry angle, or diaspora founder with European industrial network
5. Evidence of founder-led sales — founder has talked to 20+ potential buyers personally

## Hard rejects
1. Consumer applications (B2C)
2. Generic horizontal AI tools without a specific industrial workflow
3. Companies already at Series A or beyond (too late for our stage)
4. Pure marketplace models without a SaaS layer
5. Hardware-only companies without a recurring software revenue model

## Value-add
1. Direct warm introductions to procurement decision-makers at 12+ Baltic and Nordic industrial companies
2. Hands-on GTM support for first 3 enterprise pilots in Estonia and Finland
3. Baltic government grant program navigation (EAS, Business Finland, LMT)
4. Network of 30+ regional industrial founder alumni for reference calls and co-investments

## Ideal company description
A 2-3 person technical team where at least one founder spent 3+ years working inside an industrial company (logistics operator, manufacturer, energy company, or fleet owner). They have identified a painful workflow that industrial buyers currently manage with spreadsheets or manual processes. They have built a focused MVP and have 1-2 paying customers or strong LOIs from named companies. They are building initially for the Baltic or Nordic market with a clear path to expand across European industrial buyers. The technology layer is specific and not easily replicated by a horizontal SaaS tool.

## Bad-fit company description
A general-purpose AI SaaS platform targeting "enterprise operations" without a specific industrial workflow, sector, or buyer defined. Claims industrial relevance by mentioning "manufacturing" and "logistics" in the pitch but cannot name a specific job title that would pay for the product or a specific process it replaces. No founder with direct domain experience. Team has built B2C or generic SaaS products before but pivoted to industry because it sounds defensible.

## Portfolio pattern
Existing portfolio companies all have: specific buyer workflow, named reference customers, founders with direct operator experience in the target sector, and initial traction within the first 6 months of operation. The common failure mode in rejected deals is "interesting technology, vague buyer."

## What the fund can uniquely help with
Warm introductions to heads of operations, fleet managers, and IT buyers at Scandinavian and Baltic industrial companies. Specific: 12 manufacturing companies in Estonia/Finland, 8 logistics operators in Lithuania/Latvia, 4 energy companies in the Nordics. Fund has directly sourced from this network before and can facilitate trials. Cannot help with US market, consumer, or fintech.
